The RBI opens a new lending lane — banks can now fund listed REITs and InvITs

Quietly, the central bank just rewired how infrastructure and real estate get financed. The RBI's Third Amendment Directions, 2026 now let banks lend directly to REITs and InvITs — but only those registered with SEBI and listed on a recognised exchange — opening a fresh credit channel exactly as firms rush to issue bonds while rupee-support measures push borrowing costs lower. The 5.25% repo hold still anchors it all, with the monsoon now the named domestic risk.

  • New rule: banks may lend to SEBI-registered, listed REITs/InvITs.
  • Repo held at 5.25%; FY27 GDP pegged at 6.6%.
  • Indian crude basket averaged ~$110/bbl in Apr–May — now easing.
  • RBI flags the southwest monsoon as the key domestic swing risk.
Why it matters:Letting bank capital flow into listed infra and property trusts deepens a market India has wanted to scale for years — and pairing it with the listing requirement is a deliberate nudge toward transparency, channelling cheap-money demand into regulated vehicles rather than opaque private debt right as the rate cycle turns supportive.
