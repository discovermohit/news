Google ships Gemma 4 and unveils TurboQuant — the efficiency front, not the IQ front

The most interesting AI moves this week are about doing more with less silicon. Google released Gemma 4, an open-model series under the Apache 2.0 license built specifically for advanced reasoning and agentic workflows, and at ICLR 2026 unveiled TurboQuant — an algorithm that slashes the memory overhead of the KV cache using vector rotation plus Johnson-Lindenstrauss compression. Both point the same way: the race is shifting from raw parameter scaling toward efficiency-first AI that runs cheaper, including on-device.

  • Gemma 4 ships open under Apache 2.0, tuned for agents.
  • TurboQuant (ICLR 2026) cuts KV-cache memory overhead.
  • OpenAI's GPT-5.5 line keeps expanding (Pro, Instant variants).
  • Theme: a shift from scaling to efficiency-first AI.
Why it matters:Memory and KV-cache cost — not model IQ — are what actually cap how cheaply AI runs at scale and whether it fits on a phone; an open Gemma 4 plus a compression breakthrough like TurboQuant chips directly at data-center bills and on-device inference, which is the unlock that decides whether agentic AI stays a premium tier or becomes the default.
