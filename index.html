RBI keeps the repo rate at 5.25% — steady hands in a stormy week

When the world outside is on fire, the central bank chose to do nothing — and that was the point. The RBI's Monetary Policy Committee, chaired by Governor Sanjay Malhotra, held the repo rate at 5.25% and stuck with a neutral stance, judging that the US–Iran conflict and sticky inflation called for patience rather than fresh moves. Malhotra's line was that India is "relatively well-positioned" to absorb external shocks — and that this is the moment to build resilience, not spend it.

  • Repo rate held at 5.25% — the second straight meeting on hold.
  • Stance stays neutral; same unanimous call as April.
  • Backdrop: the US–Iran war and uncertain inflation path.
  • Malhotra: use the turbulence to strengthen resilience.
Why it matters:By refusing to flinch while oil spikes and equities wobble, the RBI is signalling it won't burn rate-cut ammunition on a geopolitical shock it can't control — preserving room to act if the conflict actually tips growth, rather than reacting to headlines.
