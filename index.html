Gartner: agent software spend hits $206.5bn this year, $376.3bn next

The agent line item just went from rounding error to headline budget. Gartner now pegs AI-agent software spending at $206.5 billion in 2026, jumping to $376.3 billion in 2027 — an 82% single-year leap — and forecasts 40% of enterprise applications will embed task-specific agents by year-end, up from under 5% in 2025. The signal: businesses are shifting from experimenting with agents to actually deploying ones that do work.

  • Agent software spend: $206.5B (2026) → $376.3B (2027), +82%.
  • Agent adoption: <5% → 40% of enterprise apps in one year.
  • Shift from experimenting to deploying agents that do work.
  • AI infra to absorb 45%+ of total AI spend.
Why it matters:An 82% single-year jump in agent spend is textbook inflection territory — but the number that will actually matter by December isn't adoption, it's retention, because embedding an agent is easy and keeping one that earns its cost is the real test.
