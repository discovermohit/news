Cloud, Infra & Chips
Nvidia Q1 FY27: $81.6B revenue (+85% YoY) — Data Center $75.2B — Blackwell 300 ramp drives the print. Rubin platform: six new chips, up to 10× lower inference-token cost than Blackwell, with AWS, Google Cloud, Azure and Oracle queued as first-deploy partners. The silicon layer is still the engine of the whole AI-capex narrative. Silicon · Capex
SoftBank commits €75B to 5GW of AI infrastructure in France — the build-out goes transatlantic as Europe races to host compute rather than rent it from US hyperscalers. Sovereign-scale capacity is how the AI map gets drawn for the next decade — where the gigawatts land determines who sets data, latency and regulatory terms. NVIDIA's Cosmos 3 and Intel's Xeon 6+ round out the week's hardware push. Hyperscalers · Capex
MiniMax M3 bends the cost curve hardest — built on a new Sparse Attention architecture, it cuts per-token compute to ~1/20th of prior models with 9× faster prefill and 15× faster decode at 1M context. Efficiency, not raw scale, is the story — and it's what moves million-token agents from a premium tier to the economic default. Capex · Cost curve
AI, GenAI & Agents
Anthropic overtakes OpenAI — $65B raise at a $965B valuation — the first time it has topped its rival's $852B, making it the world's most valuable private AI company. At these scales the binding constraint is capital for compute, so the lab that raises more can simply afford to train bigger. The frontier wave rolls on around it — Opus 4.8, GPT-5.5 Instant and Gemini 3.5 Flash all setting fresh benchmarks. Frontier · Capital
Pilots are finally crossing into production — 31% in Q2 — the conversion rate nearly doubled from 18% in Q1, the first hard sign the "pilot purgatory" phase is breaking. The shift is from chat to task completion across research, coding, support, legal and payments — agentic systems doing the work, not just answering questions. Agents · Adoption
Small open-source models are quietly winning narrow workflows — cheaper, easier to control, better for privacy, and often stronger than one giant general model on tightly-scoped legal, health and engineering tasks. As tuned specialists move on-prem, the regulated-industry "can't send data to the cloud" objection collapses — the biggest adoption unlock of the cycle. Open-source · Adoption
