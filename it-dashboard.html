The story in one line: H2 opens with a widening split between AI winners and AI-exposed. On Wall Street the chip trade roared back — the Nasdaq up 1.5% with AMD +7.7%, SanDisk +10.9%, Marvell +7.3%, Intel +6% and Nvidia +2.6% — while India's Sensex slid a third straight day to ~76,478 (Nifty −0.34%) with IT the persistent drag, pointedly not joining the overnight silicon rally. The services read stays cautious: TCS cut 23,460 jobs in FY26 yet plans ~40,000 freshers in FY27 (Infosys + Cognizant ~20K each), with 2026 tech layoffs past 157,000 (Oracle's ~30K the largest single cut) and 56% citing AI. In AI, the momentum is in models and silicon: OpenAI previewed GPT-5.6 "Sol," Microsoft shipped MAI-Code-1-Flash (code from plain text) to lean off OpenAI, Qualcomm opened talks to buy Tenstorrent for $8–10B, and HPE + Nvidia widened their agent-ready AI Factory. On the macro backdrop: SCOTUS upheld birthright citizenship (a Trump defeat), Iran is fighting to hold Hormuz as allies build a bypass, and India's Finance Ministry argues growth is now far less monsoon-sensitive even at 90%-of-normal rain. The one cloud: a rally this concentrated in chips leaves the tape hostage to AI earnings living up to the multiples.

Rubin platform: six new chips, up to 10× lower inference-token cost than Blackwell, with AWS, Google Cloud, Azure and Oracle queued as first-deploy partners. The silicon layer is still the engine of the whole AI-capex narrative. Silicon · Capex
Infosys deepens its NVIDIA tie-up — 50,000 engineers to be trained — the expanded GenAI collaboration sets up a dedicated NVIDIA Center of Excellence and certifies 50,000 Infosys employees on NVIDIA's AI stack. For an India services giant staring down AI-driven margin pressure, owning the implementation layer on the dominant silicon is the hedge — sell the picks, don't just dig. SoftBank's €75B / 5GW France build-out underlines the same land-grab in capacity. India IT · Capex
Qualcomm opens talks to buy Tenstorrent for $8–10B — the challenger's clearest shot yet at Nvidia's moat. Tenstorrent, led by chip architect Jim Keller, designs AI accelerators on the open RISC-V standard rather than proprietary instruction sets. Buying open silicon is how the field's number-twos plan to erode the CUDA lock-in — the one thing that's kept Nvidia untouchable. Separately, HPE + Nvidia widened their AI Factory with the new Vera CPU built for agent orchestration, a sign capex is shifting from training to running fleets of agents. Silicon · M&A
AI, GenAI & Agents
OpenAI previews GPT-5.6 "Sol" as Microsoft builds the exits — the frontier cadence isn't slowing, but the alliances are shifting. OpenAI showed off GPT-5.6 "Sol" on June 26, even as Microsoft unveiled its own in-house models explicitly to lessen its reliance on OpenAI and lower developer costs. The biggest distributor quietly building an in-house fallback is the clearest sign the era of a single dominant lab is closing — and that model pricing power has a ceiling. Frontier models · Big Tech
Microsoft ships MAI-Code-1-Flash — describe an app, get the code — Microsoft's first in-house model takes a plain-language description and returns the source code for apps and websites, a speed/cost-tuned "flash" tier. A cheap code-from-text model Microsoft owns outright is exactly the commodity layer that hollows out entry-level dev work — and the same automation the IT majors cite when trimming mid-level headcount.
