The silicon layer is still the engine of the whole AI-capex narrative. Silicon · Capex

MiniMax M3 bends the cost curve hardest — built on a new Sparse Attention architecture, it cuts per-token compute to ~1/20th of prior models with 9× faster prefill and 15× faster decode at 1M context. Efficiency, not raw scale, is the story — and it's what moves million-token agents from a premium tier to the economic default. Capex · Cost curve
AI, GenAI & Agents
Anthropic ships Claude Fable 5 and files to IPO at $965B — the safety-first lab made a raw-capability statement with Fable 5, its most capable model yet, claiming state-of-the-art results on nearly every tested benchmark across coding, vision, memory and long context (plus a Mythos 5 for trusted-access users). Days later it confirmed a confidential IPO draft after a $65B Series H at a $965B valuation that surpasses OpenAI — with OpenAI's own filing possibly landing by September. Two near-trillion-dollar AI listings are now in motion at once. Big Tech · Frontier models
Pilots are finally crossing into production — 31% in Q2 — the conversion rate nearly doubled from 18% in Q1, the first hard sign the "pilot purgatory" phase is breaking. The shift is from chat to task completion across research, coding, support, legal and payments — agentic systems doing the work, not just answering questions. Agents · Adoption
Small open-source models are quietly winning narrow workflows — cheaper, easier to control, better for privacy, and often stronger than one giant general model on tightly-scoped legal, health and engineering tasks. As tuned specialists move on-prem, the regulated-industry "can't send data to the cloud" objection collapses — the biggest adoption unlock of the cycle. Open-source · Adoption
