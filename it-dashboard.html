Top stories this cycle
Cloud, Infra & Chips
Nvidia Q1 FY27: $81.6B revenue (+85% YoY) — Data Center $75.2B — Blackwell 300 ramp drives the print. Rubin platform: six new chips, up to 10× lower inference-token cost than Blackwell, with AWS, Google Cloud, Azure and Oracle queued as first-deploy partners. The silicon layer is still the engine of the whole AI-capex narrative. Silicon · Capex
Microsoft ships MAI-Code-1-Flash — plain-English to source code — its first model that turns written descriptions into working app and website code, explicitly framed to "lessen reliance on OpenAI and lower developer costs." Every high-volume coding workload Microsoft routes to its own model is margin it stops sharing — quiet repricing ahead of OpenAI's move toward public markets. Hyperscalers · Stack
Cosmos 3 and MiniMax M3 push the cost curve down again — Nvidia's Cosmos 3 debuts as the first open "omnimodel" for physical AI, folding vision, world-simulation and action into one architecture, while MiniMax M3 cuts per-token compute to ~1/20th of prior models with 9× faster prefill and 15× faster decode at 1M context. Efficiency, not raw scale, is the story. Capex · Cost curve
AI, GenAI & Agents
Google ships Gemma 4 and Gemini 3.1 Flash-Lite; Opus 4.8 sets a coding bar — Gemma 4 lands as open weights under Apache 2.0 for reasoning and agentic work, while Flash-Lite runs 2.5× faster at $0.25 per 1M input tokens. Anthropic's Claude Opus 4.8 posts 88.6% on SWE-bench Verified, and Microsoft claims its MAI models are 10× more cost-efficient than GPT-5.5. The race is now smartest-per-dollar. Frontier · Pricing
Pilots are finally crossing into production — 31% in Q2 — the conversion rate nearly doubled from 18% in Q1, the first hard sign the "pilot purgatory" phase is breaking. The shift is from chat to task completion across research, coding, support, legal and payments — agentic systems doing the work, not just answering questions. Agents · Adoption
Small open-source models are quietly winning narrow workflows — cheaper, easier to control, better for privacy, and often stronger than one giant general model on tightly-scoped legal, health and engineering tasks. As tuned specialists move on-prem, the regulated-industry "can't send data to the cloud" objection collapses — the biggest adoption unlock of the cycle. Open-source · Adoption
Cybersecurity
US launches "self-defense" strikes on Iran — direct, not by proxy — after Iran downed a US military helicopter, CENTCOM hit Iranian air defenses, ground-control stations and radar near the Strait of Hormuz, calling it a "proportional response." Iran's IRGC vowed a "heavy response" and fired missiles and drones — nearly all intercepted. Netanyahu says Israel has halted its own attacks; Trump still claims a ceasefire is close. Washington is now combatant, not broker. Geopolitics · US–Iran
Congress rebukes the war strategy 215–208 — House lawmakers passed a near-party-line resolution that amounts to a clear rebuke of the administration's West Asia strategy. It won't bind the commander-in-chief, but it raises the domestic political cost of any move that looks like the US firing the next shot — a real constraint mid-conflict. Geopolitics · Washington

Separately, Xi Jinping made a rare visit to Pyongyang, read as China reasserting its grip on a North Korea drifting toward Moscow. Geopolitics · Asia-Pacific
SaaS, Services & Outsourcing
India hands back its rebound as the US strikes Iran — Tuesday's bank-led bounce lifted the Sensex 394 points to 73,918, but GIFT Nifty is now down 37 points to 23,295 — a lower open — after the overnight US–Iran escalation. The RBI's 5.25% hold removes one domestic uncertainty, but services valuations stay hostage to the Gulf and to Brent (now $92.27), which firmed on Strait of Hormuz risk. India IT · Markets
A US court strikes down Trump's $100K H-1B fee — relief for Indian IT — a federal judge ruled the six-figure visa fee an unconstitutional tax. No cohort had more riding on it than India's services giants, whose onsite-delivery models depend on the H-1B. The most punishing visa-cost scenario is off the table — though an appeal is likely — just as AI already pressures the same economics. India IT · Policy

The driver is explicit: 55% of 2026 layoffs cite AI, affecting 152,415 workers across 135 companies — yet TCS still plans ~40,000 freshers in FY27 with Infosys and Cognizant ~20,000 each. Specialised AI talent stays scarce even as the pyramid flattens. Macro · Workforce
