The story in one line: for the first time in months, the two lines moved toward each other instead of apart — and it was the Indian line doing the moving. On July 13 Nifty IT surged 3.6%, the sector's standout session of the year, with TCS up 5.4% to top the Nifty on the back of its Q1 beat (profit +4.6% to ₹13,349 cr, $9.5bn order book) and a fresh multi-year ABB deal handing TCS the Swiss group's entire global network as AI-driven network-as-a-service. Meanwhile the global line gave some back: Nasdaq −1.55%, S&P −0.79%, Dow −0.26%, as Brent jumped 3.74% to $78.85 on another US–Iran exchange. Note what that means — the same oil headline that sank US tech was shrugged off by Indian IT, because energy was never Indian IT's problem; demand was. Don't over-read one session: the top-5 majors remain ~46% below their 2024 peak at ₹18.15 lakh crore, 166,090 tech jobs are gone in 2026, and Gartner's $2.59tn AI spend is still growing at 47% against a total IT budget growing 10.8% — the run-the-business layer is still being cannibalised. But a 3.6% day off the floor is the first evidence in a year that someone thinks the floor is a floor. Next checkpoint: Infosys, July 23.

A supply-led boom, built for demand that hasn't arrived yet. Gartner puts 2026 AI spend at $2.59 trillion (+47%), with over 45% going to infrastructure — servers, network fabric, AI semiconductors, AI-optimised IaaS. The forecast worth staring at: AI-optimised server spend will triple over five years to become the largest subsegment of all, as cloud providers expand capacity in anticipation of agentic workloads. Wall Street is still buying it — Nasdaq +1.3% as chips rallied. But note the scope trap: IDC's $487bn figure counts AI infrastructure hardware only, and mixing it with Gartner's total-stack number is the year's most common budgeting error.

Cloud, Infra & Chips
AI servers set to triple — the biggest line item nobody has ordered yet — Gartner expects spending on AI-optimised servers to triple over five years and become the largest single subsegment of AI spending, as cloud providers expand capacity in anticipation of GenAI and agentic workloads. That word — anticipation — is the whole risk. It's a supply-led build-out betting that the agent demand Forrester keeps failing to find in enterprises will eventually show up. Silicon · Capex
The $2.59tn vs $487bn scope trap — Gartner's $2.59 trillion counts the entire AI stack; IDC's $487bn counts AI infrastructure hardware only. They are not competing forecasts of the same thing, and one compiled analysis calls mixing them the single most common analytical error in technology budgeting. Worth knowing before someone puts both on the same slide this quarter — a 5x gap in a headline is almost always a definitional gap, not a disagreement. Analyst · Forecasts
The chip trade cracked — Nasdaq −1.55% — the crowded position finally got its Hormuz headline. The Nasdaq fell 1.55% to 25,873 with chips leading the decline, the S&P dropped 0.79% to 7,515 and the Dow shed 138 points to 52,499, as Brent jumped 3.74% to $78.85 on another US–Iran exchange. Higher energy costs compress a growth multiple mechanically, which is why the semiconductor complex — the market's default risk-on expression — is also the first thing sold when the barrel moves. Indian IT, whose problem was never energy, rallied 3.6% on the same day. Silicon · Markets
AI, GenAI & Agents
Nvidia open-sourced an LLM that doesn't write left to right — Nemotron-Labs-TwoTower is an open-weight diffusion language model that generates text in parallel instead of one token at a time: 2.42x higher throughput while keeping 98.7% of baseline quality. A 1.3% quality haircut for a 2.4x speed-up is a trade most inference budgets will take instantly. And note who published it — Nvidia has no incentive to make inference cheap unless cheap inference sells more GPUs, which it does, by dragging previously uneconomic workloads into range. Frontier models · Nvidia
The $2/$10 floor expires August 31 — Claude Sonnet 5 — the model that pushed agentic coding into everyday business workflows and has been anchoring the market's price expectations all quarter — currently costs $2 per million input tokens and $10 output. From September 1 it becomes $3/$15, a 50% rise, published in advance. An introductory price with a settlement date isn't a discount, it's a land-grab; anyone whose unit economics currently pencil out at $2/$10 has about six weeks to find out whether the product survives at $3/$15. Pricing · Anthropic
64 subagents, one hour, a 50-year-old conjecture — OpenAI reports GPT-5.6 Sol Ultra produced a proof of the Cycle Double Cover Conjecture in under an hour using 64 subagents in parallel — a problem open for roughly five decades. Peer verification is pending and matters enormously. But the number to sit with is the 64: if capability scales with compute you rent rather than intelligence you train, the bottleneck on hard problems becomes a budget line. Agents · Research
Cybersecurity
140 targets in a night — and the Gulf is now in the target set — US Central Command says it struck 140 targets inside Iran overnight; Iran fired back toward Jordan and Gulf states. That's the turn that matters for technology: the Gulf hosts US bases and a fast-growing cluster of hyperscaler data centres and sovereign-AI build-outs. Regional risk has stopped being an abstraction in a vendor questionnaire and become a business-continuity line item. Geopolitics · Continuity
Hormuz: closed, or not, depending on who you ask — Iran declared the strait closed "until further notice" after hitting a third vessel; CENTCOM denied it and began further attacks to guarantee freedom of navigation. On July 7 the LNG tanker Al Rekayat and supertanker Wedyan were struck. A strait is functionally closed the moment underwriters believe it is — so the number that settles this argument is the war-risk premium on a Gulf hull, not a press statement. Geopolitics · Shipping
Anthropic restores Fable 5 globally, with new safeguards — Fable 5 and Mythos 5 came back worldwide on July 1 after US export controls were lifted, shipping with cybersecurity classifiers Anthropic describes as its strongest safeguards to date. It's a small item with a large implication: model availability is now an instrument of trade policy, and enterprises building on a frontier model are, whether they like it or not, exposed to export-control risk. The top-5 majors remain ~46% below their 2024 peak at ₹18.15 lakh crore. When the largest player cuts while reporting a profit beat, it isn't a downturn response; it's a redesign.
