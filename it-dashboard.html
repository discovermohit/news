The story in one line: the split is still structural — but the mood has flipped from Tuesday's shock back to relief. The combined market cap of the top-5 services firms remains ~46% below its 2024 peak at ₹18.15 lakh crore, with most Nifty IT names still below their 200-DMA — yet TCS opened Q1 FY27 with a genuine beat: net profit up 4.6% to ₹13,349 cr, revenue up 13.9% to ₹72,275 cr (a thin +0.4% in constant currency), margin held at 24%, a $9.5bn order book anchored by an AI-led SKF deal, AI revenue at a $2.6bn run-rate, plus a ₹12 dividend. The macro cooperated: as US–Iran diplomacy talk resurfaced and crude eased off its ~$78 spike, the Sensex recovered 238 pts to 76,742, the Nifty reclaimed 23,963, and GIFT Nifty pointed to a firm open. Wall Street rallied harder — Nasdaq +1.3% on AI-chip demand. In AI the cadence never stops: Anthropic has overtaken OpenAI on revenue (~$47bn run-rate), Claude Sonnet 5 is the default at $2/$10, Grok 4.5 is in private beta, China's Z.ai GLM-5.2 nears the frontier, and TCS inked a global Anthropic partnership. The one thing that matters now: whether TCS's steadier tone sparks a broader Nifty IT relief rally.

The build-out isn't slowing; it's spreading across geographies and squeezing more compute per dollar.

AI, GenAI & Agents
Claude Sonnet 5 is the default — near-frontier at $2/$10 — Anthropic's most agentic Sonnet yet is now the default for free and paid users, priced at $2 per million input / $10 output tokens (rising to $3/$15 after Aug 31), with gains in long-run coding, tool use and debugging that put it close to the flagship tier. When a mid-tier model performs near the flagship at a fraction of the price, the whole value ladder compresses — capability that cost a premium a year ago is now the baseline. Frontier models · Anthropic
Anthropic overtakes OpenAI on revenue — and in the enterprise — Anthropic now leads on self-reported revenue (~$47bn run-rate vs OpenAI's $25–33bn) and on business subscriptions, while ChatGPT's share of GenAI visits slipped below 50% in May. Meanwhile Grok 4.5 entered private beta inside SpaceX and Tesla (1.5T "V9" foundation) and China's Z.ai shipped GLM-5.2 at frontier quality on the cheap. Consumer buzz and enterprise revenue are no longer the same company — and it's the latter that decides which lab services firms standardise on. Frontier models · Multi-lab
The agent boom meets Forrester's cold water — Gartner pegs agent software spend at $206.5B in 2026, jumping to $376.3B in 2027, and expects 40% of enterprise apps to embed agents by year-end (from <5%). But Forrester's reality check bites: enterprises will defer 25% of planned AI spend into 2027, fewer than a third tie initiatives to financial growth, and only 17% have actually deployed agents. Intent runs well ahead of execution — the winners will be those who can show a CFO a hard number. Agents · Analyst

Whether it's a genuine off-ramp or a pause between rounds is unresolved. For enterprises the takeaway holds: geopolitical risk, not model capability, is the swing factor for H2 IT budgets in oil-exposed economies. Geopolitics · World

The chokepoint carries roughly a fifth of the world's oil. For import-heavy, EM-exposed India a barrel drifting back below $75 preserves the import-bill relief that let the RBI stay dovish — the single biggest reason its equity market found its footing again.
