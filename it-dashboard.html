AI, GenAI & Agents
Claude Sonnet 5 becomes the default — near-frontier for everyone — Anthropic shipped Claude Sonnet 5 on June 30 and made it the default for free and paid users on July 1, billing it the most agentic Sonnet yet and noting it performs close to flagship Opus 4.8 on many tasks. When a mid-tier model performs near the flagship and becomes free-tier default, the whole value ladder compresses — capability that cost a premium a year ago is now the baseline. Frontier models · Anthropic
OpenAI splits GPT-5.6 into three — Sol, Terra, Luna — the preview arrives as a tiered trio: Sol sets a new SOTA on Terminal-Bench 2.1 for coding, Terra matches GPT-5.5 quality at 2x lower cost, and Luna is the fastest and cheapest. Meanwhile Grok 4.5 entered private beta inside SpaceX and Tesla and China's Z.ai shipped GLM-5.2 at frontier quality. Fragmenting a flagship into cost/speed tiers is the mark of a maturing market — most workloads need the cheapest model that clears the bar, not the smartest. Frontier models · Multi-lab
The agent boom meets its ROI reckoning — Gartner expects 40% of enterprise apps to embed agents by end-2026 (from <5%), with the market racing to $10.9–12.1B. But the honesty is in the returns: IDC and Microsoft measure 3.7x back per $1 in generative AI, yet IBM finds only 25% of initiatives hit expected ROI and Gartner sees 40%+ of agentic projects cancelled by 2027. The winners won't deploy the most agents — they'll kill the ones that don't pay. Agents · Analyst
